Introduction¶

The TMDB dataset contains the data about 10866 movies collected from The Movie Database. This data contains following information about each movie- 'id', 'imdb_id', 'popularity', 'budget', 'revenue', 'original_title','cast', 'homepage', 'director', 'tagline', 'keywords', 'overview','runtime', 'genres', 'production_companies', 'release_date','vote_count', 'vote_average', 'release_year', 'budget_adj','revenue_adj'.

It contains the movies released from year 1960 to 2015.

The data is analysed to find the trend in the choice of movie genres each year and to check the relation between profit percentage, revenue and popularity . As part of this analysis, following questions will be answered-

Question 1) Which genre movies are most popular each year?

Question 2) How the trend of genre has changed in the following years?

Question 3) Does the profit made by movie depends on its popularity?

Conclusion¶

The above analysis shows that the movie business has grown exponentially from year 1960 to 2015, with most of the movies falling into the category of genre 'Drama' followed by 'Comedy' and then 'Thriller'. There has been very less movies in the category of genre 'TV Movies' and 'Western. Amongst the most popular movies for all years are Action, Adventure ,Thriller and Science Fiction movies. There was no trend of 'Documentry' and 'TV Movies' movies before 1970. From this analysis, we can find that the people's choice for the movie type has changed over time with Documentry and TV Movies coming into trend. Though the Romantic movies have got released all the years but has not been in most popular choice much.

On further analyzing the revenue, profit percentage for most and least popular movies, it is noticed that profit percentage is not dependent on its revenue. The most popular movie of 2009, brought the revenue of more than 2.5 billion, however, considering its budget, the profit percentage has been only around 10 percent. On contrary, the most popular movie in 1977 brought revenue of less than 1 billion, but, made profit of around 70%. It is also found that for the least popular movies, the revenue and budget has zero values for most of the years. There are possibilities that the revenue and budget data is incorrect or the missing values have been replaced with zero value . This limits the analysis to find if profit depends on popularity.
